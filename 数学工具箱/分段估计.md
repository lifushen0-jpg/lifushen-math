# Toeplitz's theorem
### The theorem content：
 > [!note] 
 > Suppose sequence {$a_n$} satisfies the following conditions:
> 1. $\sum_{k=1}^n a_nk= 1$
> 2. $\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}a_{nk}=0$ when the $k$ is fixed 
 > we can get that:$$ \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\sum_{k=1}^n a_{nk} x_k = \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}x_n$$ when {$x_n$} is convergent.

> To proof this theorem, we should cut the sequence into two parts, in one part, the sequence $a_{nk}$ is small enough, and the other part, the sequence $x_n$ has convergent.
